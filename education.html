Importance of Self-Defense Training

1. Protection:

A fundamental purpose of self-defense training is to provide protection against crime or abuse. As discussed earlier, children are vulnerable to crime, so it is crucial to enable them to protect themselves in difficult situations. Children often do not know how to react when they are subjected to violence. Some people may suggest carrying weapons, but attackers can overpower children and use those weapons against them. Self-defense training, such as karate and judo, can teach students to use their bodies as weapons. It not only teaches them how to defend themselves from an attacker but also builds an understanding of when and where to use the skills they are learning.

2. Boosting social skills:

Self-defense training focuses on fostering respect for oneself and others. Once students receive self-defense training, they also feel more responsible for protecting those around them, especially if they witness someone in trouble. It is a societal stereotype that training in martial arts promotes violence, but it is much more than just kicking and punching. Self-defense techniques like martial arts help students develop social skills, teamwork skills, and leadership skills, as well as build healthy relationships with their peers. Students also learn to respect and follow their instructors, and this respect is reflected in the classroom through their adherence to rules and instructions from educators.

3. Empowering girls:

Instances of harassment against girls are increasing day by day. Many incidents go unreported, which further contributes to the rise in crime. Running self-defense programs in schools is essential to empower girls against harassment. Self-defense training develops a sense of fearlessness and cultivates mental and physical strength among girls. Abuse of girl children is a major reason for low attendance and dropout rates among girls in schools. Such training also plays an important role in spreading awareness about the significance of empowering girls within the community.

4. Boosting self-confidence:

Self-defense classes, regardless of gender and age, help build confidence among students. These classes begin with mindfulness and encourage students to face challenges and step out of their comfort zones. Self-defense training not only conditions the body to be more fit but also helps overcome feelings of self-doubt and fear, ultimately igniting confidence in students.

5. Improving Physical and Mental Health:

Self-defense programs themselves serve as a great way to exercise. The busy schedules of students often make it difficult for them to find time for physical activity. Self-defense training contributes to achieving body tone and fitness through proper training. Exercise releases endorphins, which reduce stress, improve mood, and enhance overall well-being. For those who have experienced a crime, self-defense can also act as a healing process, allowing students to regain control over their lives and overcome trauma. Furthermore, self-defense helps strengthen the mind-body connection.

6. Learning values and principles:

Various self-defense training programs cultivate values and principles such as hard work, dedication, and perseverance. Martial arts and other self-defense techniques teach students values like courage, discipline, integrity, humility, and honor. Self-defense also teaches students to overcome challenges and tap into their inner capabilities. It is crucial for students to develop a positive perspective toward failure, and self-defense techniques instill this mindset by encouraging students to embrace failure as an opportunity for growth and learning.
